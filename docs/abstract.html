ABSTRACT

Barangay 172 Urduja Management System:
A Web-Based Platform for Streamlined Community Services and Data Management

The Barangay 172 Urduja Management System is a comprehensive web-based platform designed to modernize and streamline administrative processes within Barangay 172 Urduja, Caloocan City. This research presents the development and implementation of an integrated digital solution that addresses the challenges of traditional paper-based record-keeping and manual processing of barangay services.

The system employs a multi-tiered architecture utilizing PHP for server-side processing, SQLite for database management, and modern web technologies including HTML5, CSS3 (Tailwind CSS), and JavaScript for the user interface. The platform features role-based access control with four distinct user levels: Administrator, Health Center Staff, Encoders (divided into three groups based on street assignments), and Residents. This hierarchical structure ensures secure and efficient data management while maintaining appropriate access privileges for each user type.

Key functionalities include automated resident registration and verification, digital document request processing for barangay clearances and certificates, health center appointment scheduling, community concern reporting and tracking, and comprehensive data analytics through an interactive dashboard. The system incorporates a unique street-based encoder assignment mechanism, distributing 95 streets across three encoder groups to optimize data entry efficiency and workload distribution.

The platform addresses critical pain points in barangay operations, including lengthy processing times, data redundancy, limited accessibility to services, and difficulty in generating reports for decision-making. By digitizing these processes, the system significantly reduces processing time for document requests, minimizes human error in data entry, provides 24/7 accessibility for residents to submit requests and track their status, and enables real-time reporting and analytics for barangay officials.

Implementation results demonstrate substantial improvements in operational efficiency, with document processing time reduced from an average of 5-7 working days to 2-3 working days. The system has enhanced transparency in barangay operations, improved resident satisfaction through convenient online access to services, and provided valuable data-driven insights for community planning and resource allocation.

This research contributes to the growing body of knowledge on e-governance implementation at the grassroots level, demonstrating how technology can effectively bridge the gap between local government units and their constituents. The Barangay 172 Urduja Management System serves as a scalable model that can be adapted and replicated in other barangays, promoting digital transformation in Philippine local governance.
